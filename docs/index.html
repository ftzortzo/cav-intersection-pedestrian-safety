Handling Uncertainty of Vulnerable Road Users in Coordinating Connected and Automated Vehicles at Signal-Free Intersections

Filippos N. Tzortzoglou Andreas A. Malikopoulos
Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering  ·  Cornell University
Paper (PDF) arXiv Code Videos BibTeX
Abstract

We provide a theoretical framework for the coordination of connected and automated vehicles (CAVs) at signal-free intersections, accounting for the unexpected presence of vulnerable road users (VRUs) such as bicyclists and pedestrians who violate traffic rules. We introduce a controller that determines optimal unconstrained trajectories for the CAVs and sufficient certificates for each CAV to guarantee constraint satisfaction. When a CAV's sensors detect a VRU, an emergency mode is activated that imposes certificates related to an unsafe set in the VRU's vicinity, and a re-planning mechanism is invoked so that all other CAVs in the control zone can adjust to the disruption. We validate the approach through simulations in MATLAB, Simulink, and RoadRunner.

Contributions§1.3
  1. Expanding the approach of Sabouni et al. (2024) to allow CAVs to maintain a nonzero standstill distance when arriving at the intersection from intersecting paths.
  2. Introducing a controller that accounts for VRU unpredictability at signal-free intersections operated by CAVs.
  3. Incorporating an algorithm that enables a resequencing–replanning mechanism for the safe operation of CAVs when some deviate from their reference trajectories during the critical event of VRU presence.
  4. Extending the framework to account for measurement noise and vehicle dynamics with higher fidelity.
Simulation scenarios§7

Four scenarios, each recorded from the MATLAB / Simulink / RoadRunner co-simulation. Scenarios 1–3 share identical initial vehicle states, so any difference is attributable solely to the presence of the pedestrian(s).

Feasibility sweep of $F_i(t_i^0)$§3.1

Visual demonstration of the low-level controller: iterating $t_i^f = t_i^f + \Delta t$ across the feasible interval $F_i(t_i^0) = [\underline{t}_i^f, \overline{t}_i^f]$ until an unconstrained cubic trajectory (7) satisfies (2), (3), and (5). Even for small $\Delta t$, this sweep completes in milliseconds.

Real-time QP performance§4.7

Timings for the emergency-mode QP (44) at each control step. All barrier conditions are affine in $(u_1, u_2)$; solve time is essentially constant, independent of traffic density and the number of active VRU constraints, and fits comfortably within the $\Delta t = 0.02$ s control step on contemporary embedded hardware.

Stress cases§7.3

Extended cases that drive the emergency-mode QP toward the boundary of feasibility, where the VRU barrier condition (29), road-boundary conditions (38), steering-angle limit (40), and friction-circle SOCP constraint (53) become simultaneously active. Each case documents the configuration, the constraint interaction pattern, and the observed margin.

Individual case pages to be linked here: aggressive lateral approach, low-friction surface, tight road geometry, occluded late detection.

Perception noise & fault tolerance§7.4 · Appendix A

Scenarios 2 and 3 repeated with the estimated VRU position corrupted by bounded perception noise consistent with the Velodyne HDL-32E specification ($\varepsilon_p$ of the order of a few centimeters). The inflated certificate of Proposition 7 (radius $r + \varepsilon_p$) preserves collision avoidance throughout, with no structural change to the QP.

Alternative unsafe-set constructions§4.2 · Discussion 1

The paper models the VRU's unsafe set as a disk $\mathcal{D} = \{(x,y) : (x-x_p)^2 + (y-y_p)^2 \le r^2\}$ (22) for clarity, but the framework is agnostic to that choice: any set for which we can write a candidate barrier function $b(\mathbf{x}) \ge 0$ works. The pages below carry out the analogous CBF derivation for three alternative representations from the literature.

  • ./alt_sets/reachability_bajcsy.htmlRobust reachable set for a human agent (Bajcsy et al., 2020)
  • ./alt_sets/reachability_schratter.htmlPer-pedestrian reachability set (Schratter et al., 2019)
  • ./alt_sets/data_driven_chen.htmlData-driven trajectory forecast (Chen et al., 2023)
